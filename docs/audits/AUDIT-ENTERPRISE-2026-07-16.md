## Honest summary

The prior audits' self-graded \"A-\" is not defensible, and the gap is instructive: almost every specific fix those docs claimed verifies TRUE. I checked them. The History API router is real. truckScene, .microrow, .scene* are genuinely gone. There is no undefined --brand-crimson. All five B-series a11y fixes landed and re-measure correctly. The docs are not lying about what they did — they are wrong about what it means. They graded the fixes, not the site. \"SEO/Indexability D → A-\" was written while every canonical on the site pointed at an apex that serves a competitor-shaped Shopify storefront and returns 404 for every deep route. Nobody ran curl.

That is the pattern across all ten dimensions: this repo is well-built at the layer people read, and broken at the layer people have to measure or fetch. The router is better-engineered than most hand-rolled SPA routers; the render layer it feeds visibly breaks image layout on ~20 public pages, and I only found it by unwrapping a <picture> in a live DOM and watching the img jump from 200px to 646px. styles.css has zero !important, a coherent z-index ladder and three prefers-reduced-motion blocks; its radius scale silently collapsed to one value because --r-lg self-references a property it shadows, which is invisible to reading and obvious to getComputedStyle. data.js has a real CLAIMS discipline table that is honored in most rendered copy; it also has two different surface-area ranges for the same lab panel, one absorbent that is both carbon neutral and carbon negative, and a product that says \"Coming Q4\", \"samples ship in 4-7 days\" and \"30 day lead time\" on one page. There is no XSS anywhere — I traced every URL-controllable input to every sink — and the internal sales tool with your COGS and your 25-35% distributor margin target is served unauthenticated from the customer origin with a link to it in the header of every public page.

Against an enterprise bar the site is D+. Not because the craft is bad — the craft is frequently B-grade — but because four things are simultaneously broken and misleading in production: it cannot be indexed (canonicals to 404s), it tells every prospect its own forms don't work while leaking internal deal-routing, it publishes machine-readable OMRI/Puro.earth certification claims on products your own data file says don't hold them, and it advertises your margin strategy in the nav. Any one of those is a launch stop. The cache config is the quiet one: `immutable` against hand-bumped tokens has already shipped skewed app.js/data.js pairs to real returning visitors, and the scripts that would fix it have never been committed, so everyone reading the repo believes cache busting is handled when it has literally never executed.

Sales OS grades better (C-) because I judged it against its own bar — no SEO or public-perf penalty — and its foundations are legitimately good: one global binding per file, a load-order guard that degrades instead of throwing, no dead sections. But the July 15 consolidation introduced a compose layer that deleted the DOM ids six filter handlers depend on, and nothing in the app re-renders after boot, so the Daily Plan mission bar — the tool's headline metric, the entire point of the daily-driver view — reads 0% forever no matter how many boxes get ticked, and Reset silently desyncs the screen from storage. The tool's core loop does not work, and it has been shipped and presumably used in that state.

The encouraging read: the ratio of blockers to craft is unusually favorable. Most of the go-live list is mechanical — delete a div, delete a nav link, fix a path typo, add four header lines, commit two files. Two are not: the origin decision (which gates everything in SEO and cannot be worked around) and the certification claims (which need a legal call on the per-SKU mapping, though the correct data already exists in the file, dead and unwired). A focused week closes the blocker list. What should not survive that week is the habit that produced the A-: grading the diff instead of the deployed artifact. Every one of the most expensive findings here — the Shopify apex, the 404ing PDFs, the collapsed radius scale, the frozen mission bar, the poisoned caches — was invisible to reading the code and obvious within one command of measuring it.

## Grade rationales

### Site architecture / router - C+ [site]

The router core is genuinely good and I confirmed it: real History API pushState/popstate (app.js:1262-1337), a click interceptor that correctly bails on modifiers, target=_blank, [download], protocol-relative and /sales/*. That is better than most hand-rolled SPA routers. What holds it at C+ is that the render layer ships prod-visible breakage the router faithfully delivers: webpify's <picture> wrapper defeats `.proc-left .procimg{flex:1 1 auto}` (styles.css:400-402), measured at 200px instead of 646px on ~20 public pages; every ?product=/?doc=/?preorder= param is dropped because renderForm(kind, qs) is only ever called with `kind` (app.js:444 vs 1271-1274); PRODUCTS[id]/INDUSTRIES[id] are unguarded literal lookups so /product/constructor throws past notFound() into a blank <main>; the hero setInterval keeps ticking against detached DOM after any nav (app.js:1314); and ~100 lines of unreachable render code (kraftBag/sampleBag/bagIcon/socialProof) still ship. None of these are architectural failures — the architecture is sound — they are unowned defects in the layer the architecture feeds.

### Site data layer - C [site]

data.js calls itself the source of truth (data.js:2) and in the disciplined places it earns that — the CLAIMS table (1514-1527) is real, 'up to ~5:1' and 'USDA Organic pending, never present as certified' are consistently honored in rendered copy, and all 14 local image paths resolve. But the file has drifted into three failure classes that a staff engineer would block on. (1) Correctness: `file:"assets/spec sheets/..."` at data.js:133-134 with a space, against an on-disk `assets/spec-sheets/` I verified by ls — every ungated PDF download 404s, and because _redirects:7 is a 200 SPA fallback it silently serves HTML renamed .pdf. app.js:29-37 already has the correct path, so two sources of truth already disagree. (2) Claim drift the CLAIMS table was built to prevent and does not cover: the same absorbent is 'carbon neutral' at data.js:837 and sold on a 'carbon negative story' at 1441; biochar surface area is 215-300 m²/g at 680 and 230-260 at 153, both citing the same IBI panel; multipurpose fiber says 'Coming Q4', 'Free 1 lb samples ship now', and '30 day lead time' on one product. (3) A large graveyard — ~45 lines of bag copy feeding dead kraftBag(), ~10 orphaned keys, stale variantIds whose own comment instructs maintainers to edit the wrong file. The graveyard is precisely where the claim discipline stopped: data.js:298 holds a hard unqualified 5:1 that CLAIMS names as risky, one wire-up away from publishing.

### Site CSS / tokens - B- [site]

This is the best-engineered dimension on the site and I want to be fair about it: zero !important in 760 lines, a coherent five-step z-index ladder, every @media block matches live selectors, prefers-reduced-motion handled in three places, and the three AUDIT-FIXED claims I checked (no --brand-crimson, no duplicate .offer, no .microrow/.scene*) all verify TRUE. What holds it below B is one class of self-inflicted bug in the local alias block at styles.css:5-21, invisible to reading and only found by measuring computed values. `--r-lg:var(--r)` self-references a property the same :root overrides, so it resolves against 6px rather than tokens' 10px — the two-tier radius scale silently collapsed to one value across 21 selectors including .card and .offer. `--gold-d:var(--navy-500)` computes to navy three lines under a comment asserting '--gold* → crimson, no more misnomers', and `.lane em` (navy) / `.lane:hover em` (crimson) proves the intent was a darker crimson. Both are worse than plain bugs because they make the token file un-debuggable: fixing tokens.css does nothing and the comment lies. Add ~55 lines of verified-dead CSS and 14 hardcoded `#24478a` that duplicate --navy-600 exactly, and a rebrand would half-propagate.

### Sales OS architecture - C- [sales-os]

Judged purely as an internal tool — no SEO or public-perf penalty applied — and it still lands here, because the primary workflow does not work. The July 15 consolidation added a compose layer (app.js:794-804) that strips each renderer's <section> wrapper, deleting the ids that six pill-filter handlers scope to (app.js:232, 479; gtm.js:67, 176, 221). I verified live: none of #sec-segments/#sec-accounts/#sec-gtm-* exist; clicking a segment pill leaves both 'All' and 'Ag / Hort' lit, and #sec-outreach accidentally collides so one click strips the active state off three unrelated filter groups. Separately, nothing re-renders after boot: render() runs once (app.js:1044), toggleChk only toggles a class and writes storage, so the Daily Plan mission bar — the tool's headline metric — is frozen at 0/184 · 0% no matter how many boxes are ticked. resetChecks clears localStorage but calls go(), which only flips .active classes, so the DOM keeps showing ticks storage no longer has — a data-loss-shaped desync on a tool that promises 'every checkbox persists'. No hashchange listener, so back does nothing while go() pushes a history entry on every click. The foundations are actually good — one global binding per data file, gtm.js IIFE-wrapped with a `window.GTMB &&` guard that degrades instead of throwing, no dead sections — which is why this is C- and not D.

### Sales OS CSS - C+ [sales-os]

Layering is genuinely well thought out — tokens → styles → gtm-command-center (8 id-scoped rules) → collab (one namespace), no duplicate blocks, no leak from the public site (sales/index.html never loads ../styles.css), and the dynamically-built classes (.brand-${side}, .owner-${o}, .rep-${side}) are all live despite reading as dead. But the v5 light re-skin was never finished across the token boundary and that is the root of everything below it. sales/build-later.html:11 loads styles.css with no tokens.css — I confirmed in a browser: var_paper empty, body font 'Times', navy spine gone, the whole parked strategy half of the tool renders as grey-on-black serif. sales/styles.css:31 redeclares the tokens.css --purple primitive, so brandkit.html — the page a designer opens to answer 'what is our purple' — paints #6d4fb0 beside a caption reading #9a7fc8. The alias layer it defines at :22-24 is bypassed 81 times in favor of raw --navy-*/--crimson-*, so it mediates nothing. A parallel hardcoded status ramp at :27-31 has silently superseded tokens' --success/--warning/--info, whose only consumers repo-wide are brandkit's swatches. Plus a real visible defect: .flow-step .owner (collab.css:86) scopes the pill base away from the second render site at app.js:999, so pipeline-reporting owner tags render as unstyled bleeding text.

### Build / deploy / config - D [repo]

This is where the repo is actually broken in production. _headers:9-16 ships `immutable` on /app.js, /data.js, /styles.css — a promise the URL changes whenever content does — against hand-maintained ?v= tokens that demonstrably did not. Git on origin/main shows `clean22` spanning three separate app.js content changes and three data.js changes, and HEAD still serves data.js?v=clean25 after data.js changed while already on clean25. A returning visitor can hold old app.js pinned immutable for a year against new data.js: real version skew, unrecoverable by reload. The scripts that fix this are untracked — I confirmed `git log --all -- scripts/build.mjs scripts/stamp-assets.mjs` returns nothing and origin/main carries only check-dashes.mjs, so stamp-assets.mjs has never run on a deploy and build.mjs:4's instruction to point Pages at it cannot have been followed. netlify.toml is dead (no wrangler.toml, no package.json — I checked) yet is the only in-repo declaration of the build command, so whether the brand gate runs at all rests on undocumented dashboard state; worse, its /* → /index.html rule has no /sales/ carve-out and would break Sales OS on any re-adoption. Three internal audit .md files sit on the deploy branch at the publish root and are directly fetchable, one of them a grade table reading 'Overall launch readiness C- → A-'. tokens.css is stamped but has no _headers rule and is served under two different keys.

### Security - D [repo]

The JS is clean and I want that on record: I traced every URL-controllable input to a sink and there is no DOM XSS — location.pathname is only ever an object key against literal maps, location.search reaches only parseType() which compares against constants, the Sales OS hash is validated against NAV before use, and there are no secrets, no fetch/eval/document.write anywhere. The grade is not about the code; it is about exposure. _redirects:2 serves the internal Sales OS unauthenticated from the customer-facing origin, and index.html:55 advertises it in the header of every public page — I confirmed both. The payload is confidential: sales/data.js:57 'Price floors & COGS by product', :63 a 25-35% distributor margin target and exclusivity posture, freight-margin breakevens, per-ICP objection scripts. A buyer can read your margin gate before they negotiate, by clicking a tab on your marketing site. Then app.js:464 prints '↳ Preview: submits are captured locally (no backend). Wire to internal app, routing: ${f.routing}' inside every live lead form — styles.css:597 styles it, nothing hides it — disclosing internal deal-routing ('route to founder/carbon lead') to prospects, while the handler (app.js:474-487) does no network call at all, so 100% of submitted PII is dropped. _headers is 19 lines of Cache-Control and nothing else: no CSP, nosniff, Referrer-Policy or frame-ancestors on a site whose entire conversion path is forms.

### Accessibility - C+ [site]

All five prior-audit a11y claims genuinely landed — I re-measured each: --mute at 5.68:1, the carousel's inert/aria-pressed/reduced-motion pause, doc-link aria-labels, 24px hero dots, real breadcrumbs with aria-current. Heading hierarchy is sound (one h1 per route, slides 1..n as h2 and inert anyway), forms use native constraint validation with every input label-associated, and the success message is role=status and focused. That is real work. What holds it at C+ is that the highest-impact defect was never tested: router() replaces #app's innerHTML and never moves focus or announces — #app even has tabindex="-1" for exactly this and the skip-link handler already does it — so every internal navigation destroys the focused link, drops focus to body, announces nothing, and dumps the user back at the skip link on next Tab. WCAG 2.4.3, on every nav. The burger is the last focusable header element while the panel it opens sits earlier in the DOM, so forward-Tab skips the menu entirely and Escape doesn't close it. And styles.css:481 renders the offer line — 'Free sample' / 'Coming Q4', the sentence the whole samples-first GTM hangs on — in crimson on navy at 2.24:1, the identical failure the footer comment at styles.css:615 identified at 2.25:1 and fixed in one place only.

### Performance - B- [site]

The fundamentals are right and measured: the LCP preload URL byte-matches hero slide 0, preconnects to all three third-party origins are present, webpify transforms the string before insertion so there is no double fetch, all 22 local rasters have .webp companions with large real savings (985KB → 73KB), there are zero unreferenced assets, and the layout containers all reserve space (hero min-height 600px, aspect-ratio on every thumb) so there is no CLS from content. Transfer is genuinely modest — 21KB + 30KB brotli — and I am not inflating that into a finding. What keeps it from B is a set of intent-vs-reality gaps: loading="lazy" on hero slides 2-4 is inert because the slides are absolutely positioned in-viewport at opacity:0/visibility:hidden (which does not remove the layout box), so all four heroes download during initial load against the preload; the five below-fold homepage bag renders (~300KB webp) have no lazy attribute while their siblings at app.js:373/416/519 do; the Google Fonts stylesheet is render-blocking on a third-party origin with a serialized second hop to gstatic; the nav logo ships with no src and no dimensions so the header shifts on every load and the preload scanner never sees it; and FCP is gated on JS parse because <main> ships empty.

### SEO / content integrity - F [site]

I verified this over the network rather than reasoning about it, and the conclusion is unambiguous: the site cannot be indexed in any useful form today. Every canonical, og:url and sitemap <loc> points at americanbiocarbon.com, which returns a Shopify storefront (shopify-complexity-score header, _shopify_y cookie). /product/absorbent-pellets there → 404. /assets/og-image.png → 404. /sitemap.xml → Shopify's auto-generated index, so this repo's sitemap is never served. Googlebot crawls pages.dev, reads a canonical pointing at a 404, and drops the page — a canonical to a 404 is a hard de-index signal. Every social share renders imageless. Compounding it: all per-route meta exists only after JS runs, so LinkedIn/Slack/X see homepage metadata for every URL; notFound()'s noindex is unconditionally cleared for /product/*, /industry/*, /shop/* (app.js:1297-1299), creating an unbounded indexable soft-404 surface — /shop/carbon-removal renders 'Page not found' while emitting full Product JSON-LD for a product not on the page. And app.js:993 hardcodes hasCertification: OMRI Listed / IBI Certified / Puro.earth onto every product including three absorbents, contradicted by the repo's own data.js:167 comment and data.js:314 omri:false, with the human-readable twin rendered under 'Certified and lab verified' at app.js:370. That is a machine-readable false certification claim on indexable pages, from a company selling into regulated buyers.
